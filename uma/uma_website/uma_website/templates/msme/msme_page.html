Techno-Economic Assessment of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) in Rural Areas


Project Description

This training program was designed by Unnat Maharashtra Abhiyan (UMA) Program Cell and Centre for Technology Alternatives for Rural Areas (CTARA) to edifice and engage faculty members and students of UMA affiliated engineering colleges in evaluating the technological and economic health of MSMEs in rural areas. The core objective of this program is to train faculty and students to become effective resource personnel in the MSME sector by building their capacities in the systematic evaluation of rural MSMEs. .

Project Outputs

First Workshop - Unnat Maharashtra Abhiyan (UMA) Program Cell and Centre for Technology Alternatives for Rural Areas (CTARA) conducted the first training workshop at GIT Ratnagiri, Feb 17-18, 2020. The workshop was attended by a total of 44 participants/trainees from 12 engineering colleges from across Maharashtra , including 24 faculty members and 20 students. The participant cohort consisted of faculty and students from various engineering fields (Mechanical, Civil, Electrical, etc.) and economics, making it a diverse group, and thus propitious for analyzing the different aspects of the MSME sector. The workshop was conducted by Prof. Vishal Sardeshpande from CTARA.
