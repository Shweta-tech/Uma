UMA Application Documents


1. A covering letter- This should be a short, personalised write-up explaining the motivation and rationale behind participating in the UMA mechanism, both from an institutional and societal perspective. It should also lay down the objectives and goalposts that the institution aims to achieve and the course of action that needs to be charted to achieve them. This will vary from institution to institution, but should broadly reflect the spirit of UMA. The Government Resolutions of 13th January 2016 and 15th June 2016 should help institutions in drafting this letter. A sample covering letter is available for reference on the UMA website.

2. A participation criteria sheet- This is a formatted excel spreadsheet that needs to be filled and attached to the covering letter. The spreadsheet will help in judging the interest and capability of the institution to perform UMA activities. A blank formatted participation criteria sheet is available on the UMA website. A sample participation criteria sheet is also available for reference. Please remember that the participation criteria sheet forms the crux of the UMA application and should be used to build a strong case. In order to build a strong case, institutions need to prove that they have prior experience working in the development sector, interacting with other technical institutions, industries and government agencies, and possess the requisite infrastructure and faculty expertise.
