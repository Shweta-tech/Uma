Three Stage Empanelment Process and Timeline


Following a meeting of select private engineering colleges on 28 th December 2016, applications for UMA empanelment were received from 18 colleges. The process followed is as follows-

1. Stage I: 23rd to 29th Jan - The UMA Review Committee (Mr. Raj Desai, Mr. Yatin Diwakar and Ms. Oshin Dharap) scrutinized and scored the 18 applications received based on parameters such as experience in the development sector, experience in executing projects of inter-disciplinary nature, fieldwork and reporting, availability of seed funding to start T&DC, willingness to commit faculty time, etc. A cut-off score was decided upon and 14 colleges were shortlisted.

2. Stage II: 8th Feb to 13th Mar- The UMA team consisting of Mr. Raj Desai and Ms. Oshin Dharap visited 14 applicant colleges to assess their technical capabilities to conduct UMA activities (in terms of work done in the development sector as well as existing infrastructural facilities) and review supporting documents (project reports, agreements, MoUs, certificates, etc.). Each college was requested to send contact details of key professors who have done UMA related work as well as a brief presentation containing the synopsis/abstract and pictures of 6-7 key projects which were found relevant by the UMA team. The UMA team prepared a UMA summary visit report for each college.

3. Stage III: 24 th May- The UMA Advisory Committee held its second meeting on 24 th May 2017. The 14 college presentations and summary visit reports were put before the UMA Advisory Committee for approval. Based on this, the UMA Advisory Committee decided to approve the 12 recommended institutions for empanelment. Subsequent to this, a new GR enlisting the empaneled colleges and empowering them to participate in UMA projects and activities was released on 5 th July.
