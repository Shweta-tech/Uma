Analysis of Infrastructure for Energized Irrigation (AIEI) in Maharashtra


Project Description

Analysis of Infrastructure for Energized Irrigation (AIEI) in Maharashtra: This training programme was conceptualised by Unnat Maharashtra Abhiyan (UMA) Program Cell and Centre for Technology Alternatives for Rural Areas (CTARA) to engage teachers and students of UMA recognised engineering colleges to help in monitoring and evaluation of electricity supply in power grid.

Project Outputs

First Workshop - Analysis of Infrastructure for Energized Irrigation (AIEI) in Maharashtra at MIT Aurangabad, 27-28 January, 2020

Successfully completed a 2 day workshop focused on training the participants to use advanced concepts and practices to plan, design and carry out monitoring and evaluation of electricity supply grid for farm irrigation. Also to facilitate the trainees to apply learning from the training for performance assessment (evaluation) of existing electricity supply grid in assigned district and identify concrete interventions for improvement for ensuring sustainability of the system. A total of 46 participants/trainees (23 faculty members and 23 students), representing 12 engineering colleges spread across Maharashtra were selected for the training programme. During the workshop, training team introduced analysis of agriculture feeder using Matpower, case studies, templates, mapping the grid using Google Earth and tools for cost benefit analysis of agricultural feeder. A field visit was conducted to 33kV sub-station in Gandheli, Aurangabad and a nearby agricultural feeder. The practical procedure was exhibited in order to take readings from feeder using instruments like Single phase power analyser, which is essential for measuring electricity supply to agricultural pumps. Colleges were asked to visit a sub-station and a agricultural feeder in their district and complete the assignment by evaluating the grid from feeder with technical support from CTARA by next month (Jan-Mar 2020).
